How Virus Spread

How Contagion Coronavirus

Coronavirus spreads through respiratory droplets, close contact with infected individuals, and contaminated surfaces. To protect yourself, maintain social distancing, wash your hands frequently, and wear a mask in crowded areas.

  • Human Contact

    The virus primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Contact (within 1 meter) with an infected individual increases the risk of transmission.

  • Air Transmission

    The virus can spread through tiny airborne particles (aerosols), especially in poorly ventilated indoor spaces. Crowded places like restaurants or gyms can become hotspots for transmission.

  • Contaminated Objects

    The virus can survive on surfaces (such as doorknobs or phones) for hours or days, depending on the material. Touching a contaminated surface and then touching the face (eyes, nose, mouth) can lead to infection.

Corona Virus Symptoms

Symptom severity varies between individuals. Seek immediate medical attention if you experience severe symptoms like breathing difficulties or high fever.

  • Coughing And Sneezing

    Common symptoms of COVID-19, particularly a persistent dry cough. May be accompanied by sore throat or nasal congestion.

  • Hot Fever

    Body temperature exceeding 38°C (100.4°F). One of the primary infection symptoms.

  • Strong Headacke

    Can be one of the early signs of infection. Typically presents as a persistent, intense headache.

  • Shortness Of Breath

    A serious symptom requiring immediate medical attention. Usually develops 5-8 days after initial symptoms appear.

  • Confusion

    A neurological symptom that often appears in elderly patients. May indicate low blood oxygen levels.

  • Sore Throat

    A common early-stage symptom. May range from mild to severe depending on the case.

Prevention Coronavirus

The novel coronavirus (COVID-19) is an infectious respiratory disease that emerged in late 2019.

  • Wash Your Hands For 20sec

    Use soap and water regularly. Apply alcohol-based sanitizer if water isn't available.

  • Cover Nose and Mouth When Sneezing

    Use a tissue or your bent elbow. Avoid using your bare hands.

  • Wear A Mask If Available

    Choose medical or multi-layer cloth masks. Ensure complete coverage of nose and mouth.
